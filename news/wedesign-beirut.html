What Remains, What Is — A Performance

A white totem stands at the center, one solid, one suspended, echoing the relationship between what is here and what is not. Around it hang four delicate pieces, each one representing someone who has left or passed, yet still belongs in spirit.

Each day, one of these pieces is cut and allowed to fall. The fragments are gently collected, wrapped in white ceramic, and topped with a porcelain rose.

Over four days, four new forms appear. They hold absence, but also care.

This work invites reflection on how memory, ritual, and presence are tied together — and how what’s gone still shapes what remains.
